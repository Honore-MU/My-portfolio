Research Interests

I find research as an amazing way to enlarge my horizons in understanding global health threats. The whole process involves engaging in stating the problem, reviewing literature about the other related papers, stating a clear research method, finding and analyzing data, and finally making inferences about the collected data.

I have worked on different research projects during my high school including the analysis of the need for the call of awareness of Parkinson’s Disease in society. I recently joined the Young STEM Researchers Program at the National Union of Disability Organizations (NUDOR) to work on hands-on projects as well as navigate more about doing research and inferring concrete conclusions from complex data provided. With the assistance of Emile, my research coach, we’ve managed to complete the research project about exercise-induced neuroplasticity as a window to treat Parkinson’s Disease,an idea that I had recently pitched during the International Research Olympiad and became a finalist.
