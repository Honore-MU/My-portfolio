What I Do Fashion and Cultural Theatre

Growing up as an Intore dancer in the traditional boy's troupe, I developed a deep appreciation for the intricate and vibrant cultural fabrics, accessories, and dance movements of my heritage. I relished the opportunity to arrive early at the auditorium and meticulously craft the traditional Amayugi (ankle-bells) and Umugara (headdress) that adorned our costumes, feeling a profound connection to my roots.

Beyond the stage, I also took great joy in supporting my Inyamibwa dancer peers, carefully measuring and stitching their Umushanana dresses, which combined the shimmering Lam√© fabric with the elegant Umukenyero top. The process of draping the fabrics, cutting them to size, and seeing the final product come together filled me with a sense of pride and artistic fulfillment.

My passion for fashion and culture extended to the theatrical shows as well. I found myself drawn to the creative challenge of blending colors, textures, and silhouettes to craft stunning gowns that would captivate audiences. The rose-taffeta bodice and silvery-white accent of my designs are a testament to my evolving skills and the ability to infuse my cultural influences into modern, theatrical garments.
