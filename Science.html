What I Do In Science

Science has been a long held passion from my early stages of life. During my Ordinary Level education, I have always enjoyed working on projects such as making mini vacuum cleaners, Indicators from cabbages, and more so as to apply the courses from Science classes. I later decided to take my passion further to Advanced Level, leading me to choose Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) as my combination.

Last summer, I got involved in doing a seed germination experiment. I mainly wanted to analyze the effect of intensive sunlight on seed germination. To begin, I started by collecting many specimens including Citrullus Lanatus(watermelon), Passiflora Eduris (Passion Fruits), and Carica Papaya (PawPaw). Then, I added them in water and soil mediums before exposing them to the sunlight. Days passed and there was no change. I later learned that factors like intensive sunlight exposure to seeds in early stages of germination coupled with bad potting mix and inappropriate supply of water hinders the growth of plants. I changed the experiment to exposing new specimens in a dark and damp place with a rich supply of water and good potting mix in order for the success of the experiment. It may sound simple, but this experiment taught me that like plants that require favorable conditions for their survival, we humans also need countless factors for our prosperity. It led me to changing different aspects of life as a result.
